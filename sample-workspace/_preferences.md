# Preferences

Atlas reads this file at the start of every session. It captures how *you* like to
work, so Atlas behaves like your assistant and not a generic one. This is the sample
workspace's version (for "Sarah Chen"); during onboarding Atlas writes your own.
Free text — edit it whenever your preferences change.

## Review cadence
Weekly sweep on Monday morning, plus a quick glance whenever something is on fire.

## What "urgent" means to me
The default rule fits me: priority `high`, or a `medium` card that has gone late.
No tuning needed.

## How much to push
Surface owner-less blockers and stale `WAIT` cards unprompted — I'd rather be nudged
than let something rot. Don't nag on low-priority items.

## How to capture commitments
Eagerly. If I say it, card it unless I wave you off. Tell me out loud when you decide
*not* to card something.

## Method
Light card method (S/M/L sizing in `classification`). This is an **M** project, so the
full project-card earns its keep. I use the extended cards (risk/issue/decision/
milestone/deliverable) here because the cutover warrants the structure.

## Email
My mail lives in **Outlook (work)**. Decision on a mail→card MCP bridge: **deferred** —
revisit once the OrderHub cutover is behind us. (See README §"A question worth
answering: your email".)
